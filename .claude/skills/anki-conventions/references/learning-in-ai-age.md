# Learning in the AI Age

Why memorize anything when LLMs can answer instantly?

## The Phase-Transition Model

Knowledge follows phase transitions (John Wu). LLMs are only safe to lean on in the final phase.

| Phase | Description | LLM Role |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Subcritical** | Building foundational intuition, isolated facts | Dangerous -- AI answers feel right but don't build neural pathways |
| **Critical** | Connecting ideas, seeing patterns across domains | Use cautiously -- AI can suggest connections, but you must verify |
| **Supercritical** | Robust mental models, fluent reasoning | Safe -- you can evaluate AI output because you have the scaffolding |

**Key insight:** Use AI to *test* understanding, never to *replace* the struggle that builds it.

## What to Card vs What to Outsource

| Card It (builds mental models) | Outsource to AI (retrieval) |
|--------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| Foundational concepts and "why" reasoning | Exact API signatures and syntax |
| Trade-offs between approaches | Boilerplate and templates |
| Mental models for system behavior | Procedural setup steps |
| Failure modes and how to diagnose them | Rarely-used feature details |
| Design decision frameworks | Language-specific trivia |
| "When to use X over Y" reasoning | Configuration reference |

**Decision rule:** If forgetting it would impair your ability to *think* about a problem, card it. If forgetting it only slows you down for 30 seconds of lookup, outsource it.

## AI + Spaced Repetition Synergy

AI and SRS are complementary, not competing:

- **AI for card generation:** LLMs produce draft cards from notes; you curate and refine
- **AI for gap detection:** LLMs identify what you haven't covered in a domain
- **AI for testing:** Ask an LLM to quiz you, then compare against your SRS performance
- **SRS for long-term retention:** No AI substitute for distributed practice over months
- **Efficiency gains:** ML-driven adaptive scheduling (like FSRS) reduces unnecessary reviews 15-20% while improving retention 10-15%

## Anti-Pattern: False Confidence from AI

Reading an AI summary feels like understanding. It is not.

Signs you're substituting AI for learning:
- You can prompt for the answer but can't explain it without AI
- You skip the "struggle phase" by asking AI immediately
- Your cards test recognition of AI-generated text, not your own understanding
- You stop making cards because "I can just ask ChatGPT"

**The test:** Close the AI, explain the concept to a rubber duck. If you can't, you don't know it yet.

## Practical Guidelines

1. **Learn first, AI second:** Build foundational understanding before using AI as a tool
2. **Card the reasoning:** Focus cards on "why" and "when," not "what" or "how to look up"
3. **Use AI to challenge yourself:** Generate practice questions, not answers
4. **Audit quarterly:** Review your card creation rate -- if it's dropping because of AI reliance, course-correct
5. **Trust the discomfort:** Struggling to recall is the point, not a sign you should ask AI instead
